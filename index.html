The 2020 AFL Season

The 2020 AFL season, though interrupted by the COVID outbreak, was successfully played out. Port Adelaide took home the minor premiership after the shortened 17 home and away games, with no games being played in Victoria after Week 4. Still, it was two Victorian sides who played the final at the GABBA, won by Richmond over Geelong.

Highs and Lows in Adelaide

Port Adelaide secured the minor premiership in 2020 with 14 wins, beating Brisbane on percentage. Adelaide, the other team in the city, won the wooden spoon, finishing last with 3 wins and the worst percentage. The eventual premiers, Richmond, secured a third position, whilst their Grand Final opponent Geelong secured fourth. It was the first time in decades that neither of the top 2 teams made the Grand Final.

Search Interest on Google

Though the traditional home of the AFL is Victoria, the search interest for the term "AFL" was in fact strongest across the season in South Australia, which perhaps reflects the strong performance of the Port Adelaide team. Queensland, which played host to many re-scheduled games as well as the Grand Final, showed little interest in the competition; it is traditionally a rugby state.
